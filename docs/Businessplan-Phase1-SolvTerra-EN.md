## 2.1 Company Objectives

### Vision

**"A world where every person can make a measurable contribution to society with just 5 minutes of their time."**

SolvTerra pursues the vision of fundamentally transforming civic engagement. We believe that the desire to help exists in every person - but traditional volunteering models with rigid time commitments and high coordination effort stand in the way of this desire. Our vision is a society where engagement is as naturally integrated into daily life as checking social media: fast, flexible, measurable, and fulfilling.

### Long-term Company Objectives

SolvTerra has set ambitious but realistic goals to be achieved in three development phases:

**Year 1 (2026) - Market Entry and Validation:**
- 25-30 active NGO partners on the platform
- 2,000-3,000 registered and active students
- 10,000+ successfully completed micro-challenges
- Proof of concept for the platform model

**Year 3 (2028) - Growth and Establishment:**
- Over 100 NGO partners nationwide in Germany
- Over 10,000 active students
- Cost-covering operations through diversified revenue
- Establishment as the leading micro-volunteering platform in Germany

**Year 5 (2030) - Market Leadership and Expansion:**
- Market leader for micro-volunteering in the German-speaking region
- Expansion into the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)
- Break-even achieved, sustainably profitable
- Over 50,000 active users and 500+ NGO partners

### Key Success Factors

The success of SolvTerra depends on five critical factors that we continuously measure and optimize:

**1. User Engagement (Students)**
The platform stands or falls with the active participation of students. We measure success based on the 30-day retention rate (target: >40%), task completion rate (target: >80%), and the proportion of monthly active users (target: >25%). Gamification elements such as points, badges, and leaderboards should reinforce intrinsic motivation.

**2. NGO Satisfaction**
NGOs are our primary customers. We measure their satisfaction through quality ratings of completed tasks (target: >4.0/5), NGO retention rate (target: >80% annually), and recommendation rate. Only when NGOs experience real added value will they use the platform long-term.

**3. Platform Growth**
Organic growth is crucial for achieving network effects. We aim for monthly user growth of 10-15% in the initial phase, with a viral coefficient >1 (each user brings at least one more).

**4. Financial Sustainability**
As a nonprofit organization, SolvTerra must be able to operate long-term without permanent dependency on funding. Critical metrics are Customer Acquisition Cost (target: <â‚¬5 per student, <â‚¬100 per NGO), Lifetime Value, and a runway of at least 12 months.

**5. Quality of Micro-Tasks**
The quality of completed tasks is essential for trust on both sides. We implement a rating system, spot checks, and quality scores for volunteers.

### Strategy

SolvTerra pursues a **hybrid strategy** that combines nonprofit goals with financial sustainability. The business model develops in three phases:

## 2.2 Company Profile

### What is Our Business?

**SolvTerra** is a digital platform that connects nonprofit organizations (NGOs) with engaged students to solve societal challenges through micro-volunteering.

**Our Core Problem That We Solve:**
> "NGOs have countless small, important tasks that remain undone. At the same time, young people want to help but not with traditional volunteering models. SolvTerra closes this gap through micro-volunteering."

**How We Solve It:**
- NGOs post small, clearly defined tasks ("micro-challenges") on our platform
- Students complete these tasks flexibly in 5-30 minutes
- Verification is done through photo upload, text submission, or digital confirmation
- Gamification elements (points, badges, leaderboards) promote engagement
- Impact dashboards make the societal contribution measurable

### Market and Product Areas

**Primary Market: Nonprofit Sector**
- NGOs, Vereine (registered associations), Stiftungen (foundations) in Germany
- Approx. 600,000 eingetragene Vereine (registered associations) in Germany
- Focus on organizations with 10-500 employees (sweet spot)

**Secondary Market: Civic Tech / Social Impact Technology**
- Digital solutions for civic engagement
- Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) departments
- Hochschulen (universities) and educational institutions

**Product Areas:**

| Area | Description | Target Group |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **NGO Platform** | Web-based challenge management system with impact dashboard | NGOs, Vereine (associations), Stiftungen (foundations) |
| **Student App** | Mobile app (from MVP Basic) with challenge feed, gamification, social features, and impact portfolio | Students (18-28 years) |
| **Sponsored Challenges** | Branded campaigns for companies with CSR focus | Corporate partners |

**Value Proposition:**

For **NGOs:**
- Access to flexible, motivated workforce without permanent employment
- Relief for small but important tasks
- Documented impact for funding applications and annual reports
- Channel for acquiring young supporters (donor pipeline)

For **Students:**
- Meaningful use of 5-30 minutes of free time
- Flexibility: Help when and where it fits
- Documented engagement history for resume
- Community of like-minded people
- Gamification: Visible progress and recognition

For **Society:**
- Activation of unused engagement potential
- Strengthening of cohesion between generations
- Contribution to UN Sustainable Development Goals (esp. SDG 10, 11, 17)

**Verification System (Incremental Build-up):**

The verification system is built incrementally to reduce MVP complexity and enable fast market entry:

**Social Features - Multi-Person Challenges & Community Feed (MVP Full Version):**

The social features address a validated need: **62% of surveyed students would help more if they could do it with friends.** These features increase platform attractiveness and extend time spent on platform.

| Feature | Function | Benefit |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Multi-Person Challenges** | Challenges designed for 2+ people (e.g., distributing flyers together, team research, group actions) | Higher completion rate, mutual motivation, social bonding |

> **Strategic Value:** Social features create lock-in effects and increase switching costs. Users who have friends on the platform are significantly less likely to switch.

| Shareable Cards | Graphics for social media (Instagram Stories, LinkedIn, WhatsApp) |

> **Planned Date:** First Impact Wrapped will be rolled out for all users at the end of 2026. Goal is viral spread through sharing on social media.

### Marketplace Sequencing (Chicken-Egg Solution)

As a two-sided marketplace, SolvTerra faces the classic chicken-and-egg problem: NGOs don't post challenges without students, students don't download the app without challenges. The solution is through an **NGO-first strategy**:

1. **Before Public Launch:** Equip 5-10 pilot NGOs with curated challenge templates
2. **Guaranteed Supply:** At least 50 active challenges at launch
3. **University Partnerships:** Guaranteed student base through TU Darmstadt cooperation
4. **Manual Matching:** Active mediation by the team in the initial phase

The detailed strategy is described in Chapter 5 (Marketing).

## 3.2 Product/Service Development

### State of Technology

The digital engagement market in Germany shows the following technical landscape:

| Aspect | Market Standard | SolvTerra Approach |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Platform Type** | Web-based marketplaces | Mobile-first app + web dashboard |
| **Matching** | Manual search with filters | Filters + personalized feed (MVP), algorithm (Phase 2) |
| **Verification** | None or manual | NGO confirmation + photo (MVP), GPS/AI (later) |
| **Gamification** | Barely present | Points + badges (MVP), levels + leaderboards (Phase 2) |
| **Impact Measurement** | None or rudimentary | Basic dashboard (MVP), export (Phase 2) |

**Technology Maturity:**
- Mobile apps are still underdeveloped in the volunteering sector (only letsact, FlexHero have native apps)
- Gamification concepts from other industries (fitness, education) are transferable
- Verification technologies (GPS, image analysis) are proven and cost-effectively implementable

### Degree of Innovation

**SolvTerra is innovative in three dimensions:**

**1. Format Innovation:**
The micro-volunteering format (5-30 minutes) does not exist as a dedicated offering in Germany. Existing platforms start at 1-2 hours minimum engagement. 74% of surveyed students were deterred from engagement by exactly this time barrier.

**2. Mechanics Innovation:**
The combination of gamification and verification is new in the nonprofit sector. While fitness apps (Strava), language learning apps (Duolingo), and sustainability apps (JoJo Rewards) use these mechanics, they are missing in volunteering.

**3. Business Model Innovation:**
Sponsored Challenges as monetization are not established in the German engagement market. Most platforms finance themselves through funding, donations, or membership fees.

### Why Does This Product Not Exist Yet?

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Market Focus** | Existing providers emerged from traditional Ehrenamt (volunteering) with long-term commitments |
| **Target Group Understanding** | Gen Z preferences (flexibility, gamification) were not systematically addressed |
| **Technical Complexity** | Verification + gamification + two-sided market requires specific expertise |
| **Business Model Risk** | Sponsored Challenges require critical mass on both target groups |
| **Nonprofit Logic** | Many platforms are nonprofit themselves and innovate more slowly |
