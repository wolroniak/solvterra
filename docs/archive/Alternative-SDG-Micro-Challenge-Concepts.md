## Concept 1: Urban Safety Mapping for Women (SDG 5 & 11)

### üéØ **TIER 1 - EXCELLENT** - Proven Success Model

**The SDG Problem:**

**SDG 5.2:** "Eliminate all forms of violence against women and girls in the public and private spheres"

**Source:** [UN Women - SDG 11 Sustainable Cities](https://www.unwomen.org/en/news/in-focus/women-and-the-sdgs/sdg-11-sustainable-cities-communities)

---

### **The Solution: Women's Safety Audits via Micro-Challenges**

**What It Is:**
Women's Safety Audits (WSAs) are participatory tools where women walk through public spaces to identify physical and social characteristics that make places safe or unsafe. These audits assess safety and accessibility of cities and public spaces.

**Source:** [Women Friendly Cities - Women's Safety Audit](https://womenfriendlycitieschallenge.org/wise-practices/urban-safety-audit-public-spaces/)

**Micro-Challenge Examples:**
- Photograph and rate street lighting quality (7-point safety scale)
- Document visibility and openness of public spaces
- Verify presence of security personnel or emergency systems
- Assess crowdedness and diversity of users
- Rate walkability and accessibility
- Document gender of users in public spaces

---

### **üöÄ PROVEN SUCCESS: Safetipin Case Study**

**What is Safetipin?**
A mobile app and technology platform that collects and disseminates information about safety issues in cities. Uses crowdsourcing to analyze areas based on physical and social infrastructure parameters.

**Sources:**
- [Safetipin Official](https://safetipin.com/)
- [Urban Agenda Platform - Using Safetipin](https://www.urbanagendaplatform.org/best-practice/using-safetipin-build-safer-and-inclusive-public-spaces)

**Scale & Impact:**
- ‚úÖ **30 cities globally** (India and international)
- ‚úÖ **70,000+ app downloads**
- ‚úÖ **1 million+ photos collected** via automated driving system
- ‚úÖ **Partnerships:** UN Women, World Bank, Cities Alliance, Uber, city governments

**Real Results:**

**Delhi, India:**
- 63% decrease in dark spots from 7,500 (2015) to 2,700 (2018)
- Delhi Police reformulated patrolling routes based on Safetipin data
- Government used data for street lighting improvement program

**Source:** [Safetipin - Impact on Delhi](https://safetipin.com/the-safetipin-impact-on-delhi/)

**Bogota, Colombia:**
- Methodology transferred to bicycle audits for bike paths
- Data used to determine CCTV camera placement
- Improved safety along cycling infrastructure

**Nairobi, Kenya:**
- City County used data to engage local community
- Redesigned market and street based on Safetipin input
- Improved lighting based on community safety concerns

**Source:** [Safetipin - Data For Change](https://safetipin.com/data-for-change-delhi-old/)

### **Data Collection Methodology (Proven by Safetipin)**

**Safety Parameters Rated (9 factors):**
1. **Lighting** - Brightness and coverage
2. **Openness** - Visibility and sightlines
3. **Visibility** - Can people see you?
4. **Crowd** - Number of people present
5. **Security** - Police, guards, CCTV
6. **Walkway** - Quality of pedestrian paths
7. **Gender Diversity** - Presence of women in space
8. **Feeling** - Subjective safety perception
9. **Public Transport** - Access to safe transport

**Each parameter scored 0-3, creating Safety Score for each location.**

**Source:** [Safetipin - About Safety Audit](https://safetipin.com/about-our-company/)

**Micro-Challenge Examples:**

Global citizen science platform for biodiversity monitoring. Users photograph organisms, AI suggests identification, community validates, data feeds into Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF).

**Source:** [NSTA - iNaturalist: Citizen Science for the Digital Age](https://www.nsta.org/science-teacher/science-teacher-aprilmay-2020/inaturalist-citizen-science-digital-age)

- ‚úÖ **Used by IUCN Red List** for threatened species assessments
- ‚úÖ **Educational tool** - used in schools and universities worldwide

**Evidence of SDG Contribution:**
"iNaturalist and eBird provide a way for students to take action on UN Global Goals, including **SDG 15, Life on Land**. BirdLife International uses data from citizen science projects to compile information for the IUCN Red List."

**Source:** [The Environment Consultant - iNaturalist & eBird Tracking Nature](https://theenvironmentconsultant.com/2025/07/17/inaturalist-ebird-citizens-tracking-nature-globally/)

**Source:** [ScienceDirect - Contribution of Citizen Science to Biodiversity Monitoring](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0006320716303639)

## Concept 3: Accessibility Audits for Disabled Persons (SDG 10)

### üéØ **TIER 1 - EXCELLENT** - Strong Need, UN-Backed

**The SDG Problem:**

**SDG 10.2:** "Empower and promote the social, economic and political inclusion of all, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion or economic or other status"

**SDG 11.7:** "Provide universal access to safe, inclusive and accessible, green and public spaces, in particular for persons with disabilities"

**UN Requirement:**
"The SDGs call for providing **universal access to safe, inclusive and accessible, green and public spaces, particularly for persons with disabilities**. Disability is referenced in parts of the SDGs related to education, growth and employment, inequality, accessibility of human settlements, data collection and monitoring."

**Source:** [UN DESA - SDGs and Disability](https://social.desa.un.org/issues/disability/sustainable-development-goals-sdgs-and-disability)

---

### **The Solution: Crowdsourced Accessibility Verification**

**What It Is:**
Students audit public spaces, buildings, and infrastructure for accessibility barriers (wheelchair ramps, elevators, accessible restrooms, signage, parking) through photo-documented verification.

**Micro-Challenge Examples:**
- Verify wheelchair ramp presence and gradient compliance
- Document accessible parking space availability and markings
- Check elevator dimensions and button accessibility
- Photograph tactile paving and audio signals at crosswalks
- Assess accessible restroom facilities
- Document doorway widths and handles
- Verify signage readability and height

---

### **The Data Gap**

**Current Problem:**
"Research has studied wheelchair users' physiological strain and vertical navigation challenges in relation to ramp characteristics, finding that **problems related to inadequate signage (96% of buildings) stand out, as well as issues generated by doors (88%) or elements for overcoming level differences (stairs and ramps)**."

**Source:** [MDPI - Accessibility Improvement Index in Urban Areas](https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/15/18/13517)

**Accessibility Audit Need:**
"An accessibility audit involves on-site data collection to identify existing architectural barriers within the building. Auditors use bespoke tablet based systems to walk through sites ensuring parking spaces, ramps, stairs, signage, public spaces, restrooms are all covered."

**Source:** [Atypical Advantage - Disability Access Audit](https://atypicaladvantage.in/disability-access-audit-solutions-for-physical-buildings)
